Why do electrons flow at all?

Because the wire is metal, and nearly all metals have the magical property of being conductors; they have internal, microscopic properties that allow electrons to freely flow through them. Conversely, the material surrounding the metal of the wire, i.e. the plastic tubing and your hand, are insulators.
